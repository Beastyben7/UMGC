How Search Engines Work

In the endless sea of the internet, one might ask how Google is able to sift through thousands of results in less than a second, and deliver to the user the most relevant and popular sources. Essentially, search engines like Google use things called crawlers. These crawlers scour the entire internet to locate and find results for specific user queries. These queries are then stored on high speed servers that are waiting to deliver the relevant results to users.

Importance of Keywords and Descriptions in Meta Tags

One of the most essential strategies these crawlers use is to use keywords and links to understand the contents within a webpage. Crawlers use these keywords because it allows them to understand the content quickly. Thus, using relevant terms will allow the crawlers to locate relevant information for the end users as fast as possible. Descriptions serve a very similar function, though the description tag doesn't serve a major role in SEO, it is still shown to the user within their search results. The description serves an essential role to give the user a preveiw of a page berfore they decide to click on it. If the description is poor or non-exixtent, the user may decide to completely ignore the source.

4 Elements of Relevant Content Links

1. Relevancy

Relevancy is your user's ability to understand and relate to the content on the page. If a user is unable to resonate with the content of a page, they will likely back out.

2. Intellectual Appeal

Users want content that bring forth new and engaging ideas. Users decide to interact with your content because they are hoping to find some form of information.

3. Engaging Content

When users explore a new website, they want it to be interesting in some way. If they click on a news link, and are greeted with an HTML page of plain text, they will not be engaged, and may lose interest quickly.

4. Emotional Resonance

Emotions are an essential part of our communication, so communicating with emotions in our webpages builds strong connections with users.

What Constitutes Good Heading Heirarchy?

Heirarchy is an essential element of any design. Heirarchy tells users what the most important information is first, and separates it from the less important information. Using logical heirarchy in a website allows users to quickly scan through a website to find what they are looking for. Heirarchy is developed through the use of headings, or "h" tags, as well as through order and page structure. Understanding and using good heirarchy is an essential component to constructing websites that are as user friendly as possible.
