The Importance of Responsive Design

Surely, most have come across a website while on mobile, where the website was clearly designed for a large computer screen. These websites require you to zoom in to be able to interact with the content on the page. To fix this problem, web developers use responsive designs to allow the design of the page to change and adjust for the screen size of the user. By doing this, the user no longer needs to precisely tap the screen or zoom in. The buttons are already bigger. This promotes an improved user experience, allowing users to focus on the content of the page rather than its design.

Google Analytics Reports

Google Analytics offers a variety of reports that can help developers better understand the functionality of their website in terms of their users. While everything may seem fine from the design and development side, there could be unseen problems unveiled through these reports. Here are some of the major reports Google offers:

1. Acquisition Reports

According to Google, this type of report shows how users land on your website. It will tell you where the user came from and how they were linked to your site. This could include both natural links and paid marketing links. These reports are split between user acquisition and traffic acquisition metrics. Both of these are metrics are very similar, however there are a few differences. User acquisition refers directly to acquiring a singular new user. Traffic acquisition is more of an overall strategy that attracts users to the site.

2. Engagement Reports

These reports focus on the direct interactions that users have with your site. These reports can expalin what parts of your site are being interacted with and which parts are being ignored. It can also show user activities and their potentially resulting errors. This could give key insights into improving functionality. In a separate sense, these reports also show how marketing channels are driving conversions.

3. Monetization Reports

These reports give key insights into how your site is generating revenue. In terms of an ecommerce website, it could show which products are being sold and which customers are buying them. You can also monitor your subscriptions and get insights into your highest performing products. Finally, you can see how much ad revenue your sebsite generates.
